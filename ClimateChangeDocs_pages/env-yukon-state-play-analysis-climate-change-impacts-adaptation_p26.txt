4.3.1  Adaption Opportunity: FireSmart Plus 

Most Yukoners are familiar with the FireSmart program. FireSmart has an annual budget of 
$850,000, and has spent approximately $15 million since 1999 on efforts to thin trees and 
reduce fuel loads around most Yukon communities. The program’s primary aim is to reduce fire 
risks, but it is also a local economic development tool that provides seasonal employment.  

There are several issues, however, currently limiting the capacity of the FireSmart program to 
effectively reduce wildfire risk to communities: 

  The program’s relatively modest funding is spread throughout the Yukon and is not 

systematically focused on priority areas where wildfire risk is most significant;
